## Design Context

### Users
This site primarily serves data science beginners and working practitioners. They arrive to study concepts, revisit technical notes, and move from quick lookup into deeper understanding. The interface should help them scan confidently, then settle into long-form reading without distraction.

### Brand Personality
The product should feel calm, thoughtful, and grounded in real technical work. The user describes it most simply as a data science blog, but the tone should be more deliberate than a generic content feed: trustworthy, study-oriented, and quietly confident. The intended emotional goals are calmness and depth, with enough warmth to avoid feeling dry or institutional.

### Aesthetic Direction
Lean toward a Notion-like sense of order and clarity, but avoid sterile minimalism or overly dry academic presentation. Preserve the current editorial notebook feel: light surfaces, strong reading rhythm, restrained accents, and typography that supports Korean technical writing. Support light mode as the baseline experience, while keeping future dark mode toggle support in mind when making token and contrast decisions.

### Design Principles
1. Optimize for rereading, not scrolling. Layouts should help readers enter, resume, and stay with long-form technical content.
2. Keep the interface calm but not empty. Use whitespace, hierarchy, and subtle structure to create depth without becoming visually cold.
3. Make expertise approachable. The site should feel credible for practitioners while staying welcoming to beginners.
4. Prefer editorial clarity over product gloss. Avoid loud startup-style marketing patterns, aggressive dashboards, or click-driven visual treatment.
5. Build for tokenized theming. New UI work should preserve a clean path toward a future light/dark mode toggle instead of hardcoding one-off colors.
